Logic, Sets, and Mathematical Proof

Overview

The single biggest shift from school maths to university maths is moving from computing answers to proving statements. This module builds the logical language and proof techniques used in every subsequent module. Without it, Phase 2 courses (Real Analysis, Abstract Algebra) will be inaccessible.

Why This Matters

Every theorem you will ever prove rests on the machinery built here. Students who skip this struggle permanently with Analysis and Algebra.

Learning Outcomes

  1. 1Construct and evaluate logical arguments using propositional and predicate logic.
  2. 2Work fluently with set notation, power sets, Cartesian products, and cardinality.
  3. 3Write correct direct proofs, proofs by contradiction, and proofs by contrapositive.
  4. 4Apply mathematical induction (weak and strong) to prove statements about all natural numbers.
  5. 5Understand and negate quantified statements (∀, ∃) without logical error.
  6. 6Recognise when a proof is incomplete or incorrect.

⚡ Milestone Checkpoint

Prove that √2 is irrational. Prove there are infinitely many primes. Write proofs using ε-notation informally as a warm-up for Analysis.

AI Integration

Proof checking: paste a proof draft and ask for logical gaps. Counterexample hunting: ask for concrete examples that break a false conjecture.
