Single Variable Calculus

Overview

NCEA Level 3 covers the mechanics of differentiation and integration. This module extends and formalises that knowledge — L'Hôpital's rule, improper integrals, sequences, series, and Taylor polynomials — and introduces the why behind the techniques.

Why this matters

The foundation for every analytical tool in the programme. Multivariable Calculus, ODEs, and Real Analysis all build directly on this.

Learning outcomes
  1. 1
    Calculate limits rigorously, including indeterminate forms via L'Hôpital's rule.
  2. 2
    Apply differentiation to related rates, optimisation, and curve analysis.
  3. 3
    Evaluate definite and improper integrals using substitution, integration by parts, and partial fractions.
  4. 4
    Determine convergence and divergence of sequences and series using standard tests.
  5. 5
    Construct Taylor and Maclaurin polynomials and use them for approximation.
  6. 6
    Use polar coordinates and parametric equations.
⚡ Milestone checkpoint

Evaluate ∫ x·ln(x) dx from scratch. Determine whether Σ(1/n²) converges and find its value. Write the Taylor series for sin(x) and prove its convergence.

AI integration

Ask for worked examples at each new technique. Ask to explain geometrically what the series convergence tests are really doing.
