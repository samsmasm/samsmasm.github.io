Metric Spaces and Topology

Overview

Metric spaces generalise distance to abstract settings; topology generalises metric spaces further. This is the language of Analysis, Functional Analysis, and Differential Geometry.

Why This Matters

Required for Measure Theory and Functional Analysis (M4.1). Also the foundation for Topological Data Analysis — a growing area applying pure topology to data science.

Learning Outcomes

  1. 1Define metric spaces; verify the triangle inequality for examples (L¹, L², L∞, discrete).
  2. 2Work with open sets, closed sets, and neighbourhoods in metric spaces.
  3. 3Prove continuity via the open-set definition and compare to ε-δ definition.
  4. 4Define and prove compactness; apply the Heine-Borel Theorem.
  5. 5Define connected and path-connected spaces; give examples and non-examples.
  6. 6Understand complete metric spaces and the Baire Category Theorem.
  7. 7Define topological spaces as a generalisation and identify when a metric is topologically useful.

⚡ Milestone Checkpoint

Prove a closed subset of a compact metric space is compact. Prove the Banach Contraction Mapping Theorem. Show that C[0,1] with the sup norm is complete but not with the L¹ norm.

AI Integration

Ask AI to generate examples of metric spaces that are not normed, or compact but not Euclidean. Use as a counterexample-generating tool.
