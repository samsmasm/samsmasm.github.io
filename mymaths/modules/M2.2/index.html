Abstract Algebra

Overview

Identifies the structural patterns that appear across all of mathematics. Groups abstract symmetry; rings abstract arithmetic; fields abstract number systems. Applications in cryptography, coding theory, and geometry.

Why This Matters

Abstract Algebra reveals that the same patterns (groups, rings) appear in number theory, geometry, physics, and computer science. It trains the mind to work with structure rather than specific objects.

Learning Outcomes

  1. 1Define groups, subgroups, cosets, and normal subgroups; apply Lagrange's Theorem.
  2. 2Classify finite abelian groups; apply the Sylow Theorems.
  3. 3Understand and apply group homomorphisms and the Isomorphism Theorems.
  4. 4Work with rings, ideals, and quotient rings.
  5. 5Understand fields, field extensions, and basic Galois theory.
  6. 6Apply abstract algebra to symmetry groups, modular arithmetic, and polynomial rings.

⚡ Milestone Checkpoint

Classify all groups of order ≤ 8. Find all subgroups of ℤ/12ℤ. Prove the First Isomorphism Theorem. Show ℤ[√−1] is a Euclidean domain.

AI Integration

Counterexample generation: ask for concrete groups satisfying or failing specific properties. Ask AI to verify a claimed isomorphism.
