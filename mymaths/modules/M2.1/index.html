Real Analysis

Overview

The hardest and most important module in the programme. Formalises everything from Phase 1: what is a limit? What does continuous really mean? Why does the integral work? Be patient — most people struggle here before understanding it.

Why This Matters

Real Analysis is the bedrock of all pure mathematics. Topology, Measure Theory, Functional Analysis, and Complex Analysis all require it. It is also the intellectual turning point of the programme.

Learning Outcomes

  1. 1Work with the axioms of the real number system, especially the Completeness Axiom.
  2. 2Prove convergence and divergence of sequences using the ε-N definition.
  3. 3Prove continuity of functions using the ε-δ definition; apply the Intermediate Value Theorem and Extreme Value Theorem.
  4. 4Prove differentiability; apply the Mean Value Theorem rigorously.
  5. 5Construct the Riemann integral via Darboux sums; prove the Fundamental Theorem of Calculus.
  6. 6Analyse uniform vs. pointwise convergence of sequences and series of functions.
  7. 7Construct proofs about pathological functions (e.g., Dirichlet's function, Weierstrass function).

⚡ Milestone Checkpoint

Prove a bounded monotone sequence converges. Prove the composition of continuous functions is continuous. Prove the uniform limit of continuous functions is continuous. Show there exists a continuous function that is nowhere differentiable.

AI Integration

Proof checking and ε-δ argument critique. Ask for counterexamples to statements you think might be false. Ask AI to explain why each axiom of ℝ is necessary.
