Advanced Linear Algebra

Overview

Revisits linear algebra at a proof-based level. Jordan canonical form, spectral theorems, bilinear forms, and inner product spaces. The algebraic foundation for functional analysis and quantum mechanics.

Why This Matters

The Spectral Theorem is one of the most important results in mathematics, underpinning PCA, quantum mechanics, and the theory of PDEs. Jordan form completes the classification of linear operators.

Learning Outcomes

  1. 1Classify linear operators via the Jordan canonical form.
  2. 2Work with inner product spaces; prove the Gram-Schmidt theorem rigorously.
  3. 3State and prove the Spectral Theorem for self-adjoint operators.
  4. 4Classify bilinear and quadratic forms via signature.
  5. 5Understand the connection between linear algebra over ℝ, ℂ, and general fields.
  6. 6Apply results to least-squares, PCA, and operator theory.

⚡ Milestone Checkpoint

Prove the Cayley-Hamilton Theorem. Find the Jordan form of a given matrix. State and prove the Real Spectral Theorem. Explain geometrically why symmetric matrices always have orthogonal eigenvectors.

AI Integration

Ask AI to connect each result to its ML application. Ask for clarification on how Jordan form relates to matrix exponentials for ODEs.
