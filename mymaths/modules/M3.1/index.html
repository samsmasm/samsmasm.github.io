Complex Analysis

Overview

One of the most beautiful areas of mathematics. Complex differentiability is far more restrictive than real differentiability, making complex functions remarkably well-behaved. The Residue Theorem gives a powerful method for evaluating difficult real integrals.

Why This Matters

Residues solve integrals that defeat real methods. Conformal mappings appear in fluid dynamics and electrostatics. Complex analysis is a prerequisite for Functional Analysis and parts of Stochastic Calculus.

Learning Outcomes

  1. 1Work with complex numbers in Cartesian, polar, and exponential form.
  2. 2Define and verify analyticity via the Cauchy-Riemann equations.
  3. 3Integrate complex functions along curves; apply Cauchy's Integral Theorem and Formula.
  4. 4Classify and compute residues at isolated singularities.
  5. 5Evaluate difficult real integrals using the Residue Theorem.
  6. 6Understand conformal mappings and their geometric interpretation.
  7. 7Represent functions as Laurent series and determine regions of convergence.

⚡ Milestone Checkpoint

Evaluate ∫_{-∞}^∞ 1/(1+x²)² dx using the Residue Theorem. Prove the Fundamental Theorem of Algebra using complex analysis. Show an analytic function on a disk is determined by its boundary values.

AI Integration

Ask AI to generate Python code to visualise complex mappings (domain colouring). Ask for intuition behind why complex differentiability is so much stronger than real differentiability.
