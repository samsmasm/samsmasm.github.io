Measure Theory and Functional Analysis

Overview

The rigorous foundation for probability theory, quantum mechanics, and PDE theory. Measure theory replaces informal 'area' with a precise framework. Functional Analysis studies infinite-dimensional vector spaces — the natural setting for PDEs and ML theory.

Why this matters

This is where self-study reaches true graduate level. Itô calculus (for stochastic finance) requires measure-theoretic probability. Hilbert spaces are the natural home of quantum mechanics, PDEs, and kernel methods in ML.

Learning outcomes
  1. 1Define σ-algebras, measures, and Lebesgue integration; compare with the Riemann integral.
  2. 2Prove the Monotone Convergence Theorem and Dominated Convergence Theorem.
  3. 3Define L^p spaces and prove completeness (Riesz-Fischer Theorem).
  4. 4Work with Banach spaces; prove the Hahn-Banach Theorem and Open Mapping Theorem.
  5. 5Define Hilbert spaces; prove the Riesz Representation Theorem.
  6. 6Understand spectral theory for bounded self-adjoint operators.
  7. 7Connect measure theory to rigorous probability (probability spaces, conditional expectation).
⚡ Milestone checkpoint

Construct the Lebesgue integral and prove the Dominated Convergence Theorem. Prove L²[0,1] is a Hilbert space. Prove the Riesz Representation Theorem.

AI integration

Ask AI to explain why the Lebesgue integral is more powerful than Riemann. Ask for the connection between Hilbert spaces and kernel methods in ML.
