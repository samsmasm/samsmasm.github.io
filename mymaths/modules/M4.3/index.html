Stochastic Calculus and Mathematical Finance

Overview

The mathematics of random processes evolving in time. Brownian motion, Itô's lemma (the stochastic chain rule), stochastic differential equations, and the Black-Scholes model.

Why This Matters

The language of quantitative finance. Black-Scholes, interest rate models, risk-neutral pricing, and high-frequency trading all rest on this framework.

Learning Outcomes

  1. 1Define Brownian motion and prove its key properties (nowhere differentiable, quadratic variation = t).
  2. 2Construct the Itô integral and derive Itô's formula (stochastic chain rule).
  3. 3Solve basic stochastic differential equations (geometric Brownian motion, Ornstein-Uhlenbeck).
  4. 4Derive the Black-Scholes PDE from a replication argument.
  5. 5Understand risk-neutral pricing and martingale measures.
  6. 6Apply Girsanov's theorem for change of measure.
  7. 7Simulate stochastic processes numerically in Python (Monte Carlo option pricing).

⚡ Milestone Checkpoint

Derive the Black-Scholes formula from the PDE using a change of variables. Simulate geometric Brownian motion and price a European call by Monte Carlo. Prove discounted asset prices are martingales under the risk-neutral measure.

AI Integration

Ask AI to explain Itô's lemma vs. ordinary chain rule with a side-by-side comparison. Use Socratic mode to test understanding of martingale theory.
