Statistical Learning Theory

Overview

The mathematical foundations of machine learning: empirical risk minimisation, bias-variance tradeoff, kernel methods, and PAC learning. The why behind the algorithms.

Why This Matters

Understanding the theory means understanding the limits of ML — when it generalises, when it fails, and what mathematical guarantees can be given. Required for serious ML research.

Learning Outcomes

  1. 1Formalise supervised learning as empirical risk minimisation.
  2. 2Derive and interpret the bias-variance decomposition.
  3. 3Analyse regularisation (ridge, lasso) through a probabilistic (MAP) lens.
  4. 4Understand kernel methods and the kernel trick geometrically and algebraically.
  5. 5State and apply PAC learning bounds (VC dimension, Rademacher complexity).
  6. 6Implement and analyse gradient descent variants.
  7. 7Understand the mathematical structure of neural networks as function approximators.

⚡ Milestone Checkpoint

Derive the closed-form solution for ridge regression and show it as MAP estimation with a Gaussian prior. Prove the VC dimension of halfspaces in ℝ^d is d+1. Implement gradient descent with momentum and compare convergence to plain SGD.

AI Integration

Primary AI module. Ask AI to explain any algorithm from first principles, to generate problems at specific difficulty levels, and to critique your proofs of generalisation bounds.
