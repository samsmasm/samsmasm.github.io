# Massey University — Mathematics & Statistics BSc Course Summary

> Extracted from saved Massey University course pages (2026 offerings).
> Three tracks: Foundations (Year 1 pathways), Mathematics Major (Years 2–3), Statistics Major (Years 2–3).

---

## Programme Overview

### Mathematics BSc

**Entry:** NCEA Level 3 Mathematics (16 credits, incl. at least two of AS91577/78/79). Without Level 3: take 160105 first. Without Level 2: take 124103 or 160104 first.

**Fees (2026 estimate):** Domestic NZ$7,415 / International NZ$34,220 per year (120 credits).

**Careers:** Actuarial work, finance, banking, insurance, food technology, government, Crown Research Institutes, MetService, AgResearch, NZ Treasury, NZ Defence, Westpac, PricewaterhouseCoopers.

**Structure:**
- 100-level: 160101 Calculus + 160102 Algebra (core foundation pair)
- 200-level: 60 credits from Multivariable Calculus, Differential Equations I, Linear Algebra, Discrete Mathematics
- 300-level: 60 credits from Real and Complex Analysis, Modern Algebra, Combinatorics, Differential Equations II

---

### Statistics BSc

**Entry:** NCEA Level 2 Mathematics (16 credits from standards 91256–91262, 91269).

**Fees (2026 estimate):** Same as Mathematics — Domestic NZ$7,415 / International NZ$34,220.

**Careers:** Biostatistician, data analyst, risk analyst, market researcher, quality assurance, health informatics, survey sampling. Employers: Statistics NZ, Crown Research Institutes, hospitals, schools, private companies.

**Structure:**
- 100-level: 161111 Applied Statistics or 297101 Statistical Data Science
- 200-level (compulsory 30 cr): 161250 Data Analysis + 161251 Regression Modelling
- 200-level (choose 30 cr): 160211 Linear Algebra, 161228 Statistical Modelling for Experiments, 233214 GIS and Spatial Statistics
- 300-level (choose 60 cr): Bayesian Computational Statistics, Biostatistics, Data Mining, Reproducible Visualisation for Statistics

## Year 1 — Foundation Courses

### 160101 Calculus
**Level:** 100 | **Credits:** 15 | **Semester:** Sem 2 (Auckland, Distance, Manawatu)

**Description:** Differentiation and integration of single-variable functions, differential equations, numerical methods, power series. Core course for Mathematics, Physics, Food Technology, Engineering.

**Prerequisites:** NCEA Level 3 Maths (16 externally assessed credits, incl. at least two of AS91577/78/79); or pass 160103/160105/160131/160132.

**Restrictions:** 160112, 160133, 228172

**Learning Outcomes:**
1. Calculate limits, derivatives, integrals, and power series of single-variable functions.
2. Apply differentiation and integration to a range of applications.
3. Apply numerical methods for approximating solutions and definite integrals.
4. Use computer software (MATLAB) for graphical, numerical, and symbolic exploration.
5. Communicate mathematical arguments in correct notation.

**Assessment:** 3 Tests (25% each) + Centrally scheduled exam (25%)

**Textbook:**
- **COMPULSORY:** *Calculus: Early Transcendentals, International Metric Edition* — James Stewart, 9th ed — ISBN 9780357113516 — Cengage Learning

---

### 160102 Algebra
**Level:** 100 | **Credits:** 15 | **Semester:** Sem 1 (Auckland, Distance, Manawatu)

**Description:** Linear algebra fundamentals — vector and matrix algebra, lines and planes, projections, Gaussian elimination, eigenvectors, complex numbers. Pair with 160101 as the foundation for further Mathematics study.

**Prerequisites:** NCEA Level 3 Maths (16 credits, incl. one of AS91577/78/79); or pass 160103/160105/160131/160132.

**Restrictions:** 160112, 160133, 228172

**Learning Outcomes:**
1. Solve systems of linear equations; perform vector and matrix calculations.
2. Use vectors for lines and planes in 3D.
3. Calculate determinants, eigenvalues, eigenvectors and apply them.
4. Demonstrate proficiency with complex number algebra and geometry.
5. Use MATLAB for matrix calculations and systems of equations.
6. Communicate mathematical arguments correctly.

**Assessment:** 3 Tests (25% each) + Centrally scheduled exam (25%)

**Textbooks:**
- **COMPULSORY:** *Linear Algebra: A Modern Introduction* — David Poole, 4th ed — ISBN 9781285463247 — Cengage *(pack ISBN 9780170291361 bundles Calculus 9e)*
- **COMPULSORY (alternative):** Same title, 5th ed — ISBN 9798214405896 — Cengage *(either edition accepted)*

### 160104 Introductory Mathematics for Science
**Level:** 100 | **Credits:** 15 | **Semester:** Sem 2 + Summer School (Auckland, Distance, Manawatu)

**Description:** Entry-level course for students without NCEA Level 3. Number, algebra, trigonometry, curve sketching applied to science contexts.

**Prerequisites:** None (entry-level pathway).

**Restrictions:** 124103, 160101, 160102, 160103, 160105, 160111, 160112, 160131–133, 228171–172

**Learning Outcomes:**
1. Apply number concepts to science (order of operations, fractions, unit conversion, estimation).
2. Apply algebra and trigonometry including exponential and logarithmic functions.
3. Draw and interpret graphs.
4. Use spreadsheet software (Excel) for exploration.
5. Communicate solutions using correct SI units and mathematical notation.

**Assessment:** Written Assignment (20%) + 3 Tests (20% each) + Centrally scheduled exam (20%)

**Textbook:**
- **RECOMMENDED (out of print — source secondhand):** *Maths for Science* — Jordan, Ross, Murphy, 1st ed — ISBN 9780199644964 — Oxford University Press

### 160105 Methods of Mathematics
**Level:** 100 | **Credits:** 15 | **Semester:** Sem 1 + Summer School (Auckland, Distance, Manawatu)

**Description:** Bridge course for students with NCEA Level 2 Mathematics. Algebraic skills, functions and graphs, introduction to calculus. Prepares for 160101/160102.

**Prerequisites:** NCEA Level 2 Maths (16 credits); or pass 160104/124103/247002.

**Restrictions:** 160101, 160102, 160103, 160111, 160112, 160131–133, 228171–172

**Learning Outcomes:**
1. Solve quadratic equations and linear systems of two unknowns.
2. Check solutions to linear, algebraic, discrete, and differential equations.
3. Apply properties of trigonometric, logarithmic, and exponential functions.
4. Calculate derivatives and integrals.
5. Use appropriate technology for mathematical calculations.
6. Communicate solutions in correct notation.

**Assessment:** Multiple Tests (75%) + Centrally scheduled exam (25%) *(exact test breakdown varies by delivery mode)*

**Textbook:** None set.

### 161111 Applied Statistics
**Level:** 100 | **Credits:** 15 | **Semester:** Sem 1 & 2 + Summer School (Auckland, Distance, Manawatu)

**Description:** Statistical literacy course for students without a strong computing or maths background. Uses Excel and RStudio. Covers data collection, confidence intervals, hypothesis testing, regression. Entry point to the Statistics major for students without NCEA Level 3 Maths.

**Prerequisites:** NCEA Level 2 Maths (16 credits); or any Massey 100-level maths (160.xxx) or 124103.

**Restrictions:** 115101, 161101, 161120, 161122, 161130, 161140

**Learning Outcomes:**
1. Use software to display and analyse data.
2. Identify appropriate data collection techniques.
3. Construct and interpret confidence intervals.
4. Perform hypothesis tests for evidence-based decisions.
5. Draw inferences about relationships between variables.
6. Communicate statistical results in context.

**Assessment:** 3 Tests (2% each) + 2 Written Assignments (17% each) + Centrally scheduled exam (60%)

**Textbook:** None set.

### 297101 Statistical Data Science
**Level:** 100 | **Credits:** 15 | **Semester:** Sem 2 (Auckland, Distance, Manawatu)

**Description:** Introduction to programming and statistics for data transformation, visualisation, and modelling. Heavy computing focus using modern reproducible tools. Allows direct entry to all 200-level statistics courses (unlike 161111 which recommends 161250 first).

**Prerequisites:** NCEA Level 3 Maths (16 credits from 91573–91579, 91587); or any 100-level maths (160.xxx) except 160104.

**Restrictions:** 161122

**Learning Outcomes:**
1. Write programs to summarise and visualise complex data sets reproducibly.
2. Recognise tidy data; write code for data wrangling.
3. Analyse and interpret relationships between variables.
4. Use statistical models for inference; interpret in context.
5. Critique data collection processes and their influence on inference.

**Assessment:** 3 Written Assignments (20% each) + Portfolio (40%)

**Textbook:** None set.

### 124103 Biophysical Principles
**Level:** 100 | **Credits:** 15 | **Semester:** Sem 1 (Distance, Manawatu only)

**Description:** Physics and foundational mathematics for biology/veterinary science students. Motion, forces, fluids, heat, waves, spreadsheets. Also covers number, algebra, trigonometry, SI units for students without NCEA Level 2 Maths. Compulsory labs.

**Prerequisites:** None (open entry, gateway for students without Level 2 Maths).

**Restrictions:** 160101, 160102, 160103–105, 160111–112, 160132–133, 124100, 124104, 124105, 124111

**Note:** Domestic/onshore international students applying for BVSc must take the Manawatu internal offering (distance = ineligible for BVSc).

**Learning Outcomes:**
1. Explain physical and mathematical principles underlying biological phenomena.
2. Solve quantitative problems in number, algebra, trigonometry, and transcendental functions.
3. Solve qualitative problems in motion, forces, fluids, heat, and waves.
4. Apply unit conversion, graphing, and basic spreadsheets.
5. Carry out experimental investigations and apply statistical reasoning.

**Assessment:** Participation (compulsory, 0%) + Portfolio (100%)

**Textbook:**
- **HIGHLY RECOMMENDED:** *Introduction to Biological Physics for the Health and Life Sciences* — Franklin et al, 2nd ed — ISBN 9781118934500 — Wiley

## Years 2–3 — Mathematics Major Courses

### 160203 Multivariable Calculus
**Level:** 200 | **Credits:** 15 | **Semester:** Sem 1 (Auckland, Distance, Manawatu)

**Prerequisites:** One of 160101, 160112, 160133, 228172

**Description:** Extends 100-level calculus to infinite series, vector-valued functions, and functions of multiple variables. Convergence of power series, partial derivatives, double/triple integrals, line and surface integrals.

**Learning Outcomes:**
1. Apply convergence tests for sequences and series.
2. Evaluate indeterminate forms.
3. Use parametric equations and polar coordinates.
4. Apply calculus of vectors in 2D and 3D including motion applications.
5. Use partial derivatives, gradient, chain rule, Lagrange multipliers.
6. Evaluate double and triple integrals in various coordinate systems for volumes, surface areas, masses.
7. Work with line integrals and apply Green's Theorem.

**Assessment:** Written Assignment (25%) + 2 Tests (25% each) + Centrally scheduled exam (25%)

**Textbook:**
- **COMPULSORY:** *Calculus: Early Transcendentals, International Metric Edition* — James Stewart, 9th ed — ISBN 9780357113516 — Cengage Learning *(same text as 160101 Calculus)*

---

### 160204 Differential Equations I
**Level:** 200 | **Credits:** 15 | **Semester:** Sem 2 (Auckland, Distance, Manawatu)

**Prerequisites:** One of 160101, 160112, 160133, 228172

**Description:** Analytical and numerical methods for ordinary differential equations (ODEs). Exact solution methods, systems of ODEs, matrix methods, phase plane techniques, applications, numerical methods.

**Learning Outcomes:**
1. Apply exact and numerical solution methods to ODEs.
2. Solve linear systems of ODEs and higher-order ODEs.
3. Construct phase portraits; describe nature and stability of critical points.
4. Formulate mathematical models of real-world problems using ODEs.
5. Use software for numerical and analytical investigation of ODEs.

**Assessment:** Written Assignment (25%) + 2 Tests (25% each) + Centrally scheduled exam (25%)

**Textbooks:**
- **COMPULSORY:** *Differential Equations with Boundary-Value Problems* — Zill & Wright, 9th ed (2017) — ISBN 9781337559881 — Cengage
- **RECOMMENDED:** *An Introduction to Ordinary Differential Equations* — James C. Robinson (2004) — ISBN 9780521533911 — Cambridge University Press
- **RECOMMENDED:** *Differential Equations* — Blanchard, Devaney, Hall, 4th ed — ISBN 9781133109037 — Cengage

### 160211 Linear Algebra
**Level:** 200 | **Credits:** 15 | **Semester:** Sem 2 (Auckland, Distance, Manawatu)

**Prerequisites:** One of 160102, 160112, 160133, 228172

**Description:** Vector spaces, linear transformations, matrix representation, inner product spaces, isometries, least squares, generalised inverse, eigen theory, quadratic forms, norms, numerical methods.

**Learning Outcomes:**
1. Explain linear independence, basis, dimension of vector spaces.
2. Use methods for solving systems of linear equations.
3. Find matrix representation of a linear transformation in any pair of bases.
4. Decompose matrices for numerical computation.
5. Compute vector and matrix norms.
6. Explain eigen-problems.
7. Explain basic properties of quadratic forms.
8. Use software for graphical and numerical investigation.

**Assessment:** Written Assignment (25%) + 2 Tests (25% each) + Centrally scheduled exam (25%)

**Textbook:** None set.

*Also required in the Statistics Major.*

**Learning Outcomes:**

3. Analyse problems using critical thinking in a mathematical setting.

**Assessment:** Written Assignment (25%) + 2 Tests (25% each) + Centrally scheduled exam (25%)

### 160302 Modern Algebra
**Level:** 300 | **Credits:** 15 | **Semester:** Sem 1 (Auckland, Distance, Manawatu)

**Prerequisites:** 160211 or 160212

**Description:** Group theory — basic properties, permutation groups, finite abelian groups, cosets, normal subgroups, homomorphism theorems, representation. Ring theory — integral domains, fields, ideals, homomorphism theorems, factorisation, extension fields.

**Learning Outcomes:**
1. Discuss, explain, and utilise basic group structures and homomorphism theorems.
2. Describe and discuss basic ring properties, polynomial rings, irreducibility, integral domains, ideals, and basic field extensions.

**Assessment:** Written Assignment (25%) + 2 Tests (25% each) + Centrally scheduled exam (25%)

**Textbook:**
- **RECOMMENDED:** *Contemporary Abstract Algebra* — Joseph A. Gallian, 10th ed — ISBN 9780367651787 — Chapman and Hall / CRC Press

**Learning Outcomes:**

3. Compute generating functions and apply to combinatorial problems.

5. Communicate mathematical arguments in correct notation.

### 160318 Differential Equations II
**Level:** 300 | **Credits:** 15 | **Semester:** Sem 1 (Auckland, Distance, Manawatu)

**Prerequisites:** 160204 (Differential Equations I)

**Description:** Advanced ODEs and PDEs. Power series and Frobenius series, special functions, Fourier series, Sturm-Liouville problems, nonlinear dynamics. PDEs: classification, separation of variables, Fourier transforms, explicit and implicit numerical schemes.

**Learning Outcomes:**
1. Construct power series and Frobenius series solutions to ODEs.
2. Use Sturm-Liouville solutions to construct Fourier series.
3. Analyse nonlinear dynamics of ODE systems.
4. Use separation of variables for hyperbolic, elliptic, and parabolic PDE boundary value problems.
5. Construct Fourier transforms; use them to solve PDEs.
6. Apply explicit and implicit schemes for numerical PDE solving.

**Assessment:** Written Assignment (25%) + 2 Tests (25% each) + Centrally scheduled exam (25%)

**Textbook:**
- **RECOMMENDED:** *Differential Equations with Boundary Value Problems* — Zill & Wright, 9th ed (2017) — ISBN 9781337559881 — Brooks Cole *(same text as 160204)*

## Years 2–3 — Statistics Major Courses

### 161250 Data Analysis
**Level:** 200 | **Credits:** 15 | **Semester:** Sem 1 + Summer School (Auckland, Distance, Manawatu)

**Prerequisites:** Any 161.1xx or 297101

**Description:** Foundational statistical concepts for students from any discipline. Variation, inference, Central Limit Theorem, hypothesis testing, statistical modelling, model evaluation. Uses statistical software.

**Note:** Must obtain at least 35% in the final exam to pass.

**Learning Outcomes:**
1. Describe sources of variation, statistical inference objectives, and the Central Limit Theorem.
2. Describe data types and random variables; summarise with statistics and graphics.
3. Implement and interpret Student's t-tests and chi-square tests.
4. Use statistical software for a range of models; summarise and interpret results.
5. Evaluate parametric assumptions; apply transformations or nonparametric tests where needed.

**Assessment:** Written Assignments (60%) + Centrally scheduled exam (40%)

**Textbook:** None set.

---

### 161251 Regression Modelling
**Level:** 200 | **Credits:** 15 | **Semester:** Sem 2 (Auckland, Distance, Manawatu)

**Prerequisites:** Any 161.1xx or 297101

**Description:** Simple and multiple regression, linear models with categorical variables, model diagnostics, inference for linear models, polynomial regression, time dependence, variable selection, nonlinear and weighted regression.

**Learning Outcomes:**
1. Explore and describe quantitative and categorical data and their interrelationships.
2. Develop regression models, make inferences about parameters, interpret in context.
3. Critically assess model adequacy; propose alternatives.
4. Create and interpret ANOVA tables; test hypotheses about parameters.
5. Compare models and select explanatory variable subsets.
6. Use statistical software for data exploration and model fitting.

**Assessment:** 2 Written Assignments (25% each) + Test (25%) + Centrally scheduled exam (25%)

**Textbook:** None set.

### 161228 Statistical Modelling for Experiments
**Level:** 200 | **Credits:** 15 | **Semester:** Sem 1 (Distance, Manawatu)

**Prerequisites:** Any 161.1xx, or 297101, or 228271

5. Interpret ANOVA tables and statistical summaries in experimental context.

**Assessment:** 2 Written Assignments (30%, 25%) + Written Assignment (25%) + Portfolio (20%)

**Textbook:** None set.

### 161331 Biostatistics
**Level:** 300 | **Credits:** 15 | **Semester:** Sem 1 (Distance, Manawatu)

**Prerequisites:** One of: 161250, 161251, 161220, 161221

**Description:** Statistical techniques for biological and medical data. Nonlinear regression, linear models for binary and count data, mixed effects models. Case-study approach with heavy use of statistical software.

**Restrictions:** 161327, 161778

**Note:** Requires access to a Windows PC and approved statistics package.

**Learning Outcomes:**
1. Choose methods for linear, polynomial, and generalised additive models.
2. Diagnose and fix problems such as multicollinearity.
3. Identify and fit generalised linear models for binary and count data.
4. Fit random and mixed-effects models for hierarchical sampling designs.
5. Evaluate and compare goodness-of-fit for fitted models.

**Assessment:** 2 Written Assignments (25% each) + Test (25%) + Centrally scheduled exam (25%)

**Textbook:** None set.
