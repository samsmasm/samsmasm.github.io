Intro to the internal assessment
Class one

The big big big picture
A mandatory portfolio of three commentaries based on published news articles, demonstrating the application of economic theory to real-world situations.
• Weighting:
    ◦ 30% of the final grade for Standard Level (SL).
    ◦ 20% of the final grade for Higher Level (HL).
• Recommended Time: Approximately 20 teaching hours.

Structural overview
• Three Units: Each commentary must cover a different unit of the syllabus:
    ◦ Microeconomics.
    ◦ Macroeconomics.
    ◦ he Global Economy.
• Exclusion: Introduction to Economics cannot be used for a commentary.
• Word Count: Maximum 800 words per commentary.

OK, but what am I actually doing???!!!
Analyze and Apply: Select a contemporary news article and analyze the described economic issue by applying relevant models and theory through the lens of one specific IB key concept.
Evaluate Policies: Provide a balanced, reasoned evaluation of a potential policy solution - either one mentioned in the article or an original suggestion arising from the context - to determine its effectiveness and impact on stakeholders.
Maintain Focus: Ensure all analytical points and evaluative judgments remain strictly anchored in the article's evidence, your chosen concept, and established economic theory.

Article selection rules
• Authentic Sources: Articles must be from published news media (newspapers, journals, or internet sites).
• No Broadcasts: Television or radio transcripts are not permitted.
• Currency: Articles must be published no earlier than one year before the commentary is written. (I suggest within the last 6 months of now.)
• Diversity: You must use a different source for each of the three commentaries.
• Ownership: Students must select their own articles; they cannot be provided by a teacher.

Structure? Something like…
Section
Target Word Count
Essential Elements
1. Introduction
≈100 words
Summarize the article's context, state the real-world issue, define 1–2 core terms, and introduce the Key Concept.
2. Economic Issue & Diagram
≈100 words
Detail the underlying economic problem (e.g., market failure or recessionary gap) and insert your primary diagram.
3. Diagram Explanation
≈150–200 words
Treat the reader as a non-expert. Use step-by-step logic to explain curve shifts, changes in equilibrium, and the resulting price/quantity outcomes.
4. Deeper Analysis
≈100–150 words
Integrate specific data from the article. Discuss elasticities (PED/PES), second-round effects, and the "economic logic" of the events. Perhaps another diagram.
5. Evaluation
≈200–300 words
Apply the CLASPP framework. Offer a balanced argument regarding policy effectiveness, stakeholder impacts, and short-term vs. long-term consequences.
6. Conclusion
2–3 sentences
Provide a final reasoned judgment and explicitly connect the outcome back to your chosen Key Concept.

CLASPP?
• Conclusion (C): Provide a final, definitive judgment on the policy's overall effectiveness.
• Long-term vs. Short-term (L): Distinguish between immediate impacts and delayed consequences over time.
• Assumptions (A): Critique whether theoretical assumptions, such as ceteris paribus or consumer rationality, hold true in the article's context.
• Stakeholders (S): Identify the "winners and losers" by evaluating impacts on consumers, producers, and the government.
• Priorities (P): Determine which economic objective is most critical in this specific real-world scenario.
• Pros and Cons (P): Explicitly weigh the advantages of the situation against the disadvantages.
Use at least three components to ensure a multidimensional, level-7 evaluation.

Key concept
Bold for Clarity: Bold the concept every time you use it to ensure the examiner cannot miss your application
Economic Context: Always use the concept in a strictly economic sense (e.g., referring to "intervention" as a specific policy tool to correct market failure) rather than its general dictionary meaning
Explain the "Why": Use the concept to explain the motivation behind the events in your diagram, such as why a government decided to step into a market
Connect to Conclusion: Reiterate how the concept summarizes the final outcome of the policy or economic event in your closing sentences
