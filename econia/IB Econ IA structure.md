# Suggested Economics IA Structure

See an example that uses this structure here: [KZ Micro](https://drive.google.com/file/d/1chXv63FLKii1vKTVPkssgcTWWsu9D4Av/view)

### **Section 1: Introduction and Terminology**

* Identify the article title, source, and date of publication.  
* Define the core economic terms relevant to the specific market (e.g., negative externalities, market failure, or price elasticity of demand).  
* Define the key concept (e.g., intervention or change) that will guide your analysis and explain how it fits.  
* **Word count: \~75 words.**

### **Section 2: Problem and Solution Overview**

* Summarize the specific economic problem identified in the article (e.g., overconsumption of sugar leading to health costs).  
* Briefly state the proposed or implemented policy solution (e.g., an indirect tax to internalize the externality).  
* **Word count: \~50 words.**

### **Section 3: Baseline Diagram and Analysis**

* Insert your first diagram showing the initial market state or the market failure.  
* Explain the diagram by referencing specific curves (e.g., MPC, MSC) and labels (P1, Q1).  
* Integrate definitions naturally within your explanation to show how the theory applies to the article's data.  
* **Word count: \~175 words.**

### **Section 4: Policy Diagram and Analysis**

* Insert your second diagram illustrating the impact of the policy or the shift in the market.  
* Explain the mechanics of the change: show how the intervention moves the market toward a new equilibrium or the socially optimum level of output (Q\*).  
* Refer to specific numbers or quotes from the text to justify the shifts shown in your diagram.  
* **Word count: \~175 words.**

### **Section 5: Evaluation and Synthesis (CLASPP)**

Provide a multidimensional evaluation using at least three of these components. This section will likely require multiple paragraphs.

* **Conclusion (C):** Provide a final, definitive judgment on the policy's overall effectiveness.  
* **Long-term vs. Short-term (L):** Distinguish between immediate impacts and delayed consequences over time.  
* **Assumptions (A):** Critique whether theoretical assumptions, such as ceteris paribus or consumer rationality, hold true in the article's context.  
* **Stakeholders (S):** Identify the "winners and losers" by evaluating impacts on consumers, producers, and the government.  
* **Priorities (P):** Determine which economic objective is most critical in this specific real-world scenario.  
* **Pros and Cons (P):** Explicitly weigh the advantages of the situation against the disadvantages.  
* **Word count: \~275 words.**

### **Section 6: Conclusion (C)**

* Provide a definitive judgment on whether the policy is the most effective way to solve the problem described.  
* Link your final thought back to the key concept mentioned in the introduction.  
* **Word count: \~50 words.**
