# Appendices: Presentation of Financial Statements  
   
Where profit and loss accounts, balance sheets, cash flow forecasts, and budgets are given in case studies or examination questions, they will be presented in the format shown below. Students should be familiar with these layouts.   
   
* A profit and loss account is also known as an income statement.  
* The term to be used in assessment for the profit and loss account is **statement of profit or loss**.  
* The term to be used in assessment for the balance sheet is **statement of financial position**.

## 4. Formulae for External Assessment  
   
### Profitability Ratios (SL/HL)  
* **Gross profit margin:** $$\frac{\text{gross profit}}{\text{sales revenue}} \times 100$$  
* **Profit margin:** $$\frac{\text{profit before interest and tax}}{\text{sales revenue}} \times 100$$  
* **Return on capital employed (ROCE):** $$\frac{\text{profit before interest and tax}}{\text{capital employed}} \times 100$$  
    * *where capital employed = non-current liabilities + equity*  
   
### Liquidity Ratios (SL/HL)  
* **Current ratio:** $$\frac{\text{current assets}}{\text{current liabilities}}$$  
* **Acid test (quick) ratio:** $$Acid~test(quick)ratio=\frac{current~assets-stock}{current~liabilities}$$  
   
### Efficiency Ratios (HL only)  
* **Stock turnover (number of times):** $$\frac{\text{cost of sales}}{\text{average stock}}$$  
* **Stock turnover (number of days):** $$\frac{\text{average stock}}{\text{cost of sales}} \times 365$$  
    * *where average stock =* $\frac{\text{opening stock + closing stock}}{2}$  
* **Debtor days ratio:** $$\frac{\text{debtors}}{\text{total sales revenue}} \times 365$$  
* **Creditor days ratio:** $$\frac{\text{creditors}}{\text{cost of sales}} \times 365$$  
* **Gearing ratio:** $$\frac{\text{non-current liabilities}}{\text{capital employed}} \times 100$$  
   
### Investment Appraisal & Productivity  
* **Average rate of return (ARR) [SL/HL]:** $$\frac{(\text{total returns} - \text{capital cost}) \div \text{years of use}}{\text{capital cost}} \times 100$$  
* **Net present value (NPV) [HL only]:** $\text{present values of return - original cost}$  
* **Capacity utilization rate [HL only]:** $$\frac{\text{actual output}}{\text{productive capacity}} \times 100$$  
* **Productivity rate [HL only]:** $$\frac{\text{total output}}{\text{total input}} \times 100$$
