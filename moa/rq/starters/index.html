RQ Starters

A Research Question needs to demand an argument, not just a description. These starters are designed to push your question in the right direction. Choose one that fits your topic and ask yourself: can two smart people reach different conclusions? If yes, you have a Research Question.

Question Starter Description / Historical Application
To what extent was... Measures how much one specific event or trend (like the rise of the internet) caused a change compared to other factors.
Assess the role of... Analyzes the importance of a specific leader, organisation, or movement in shaping events during this period.
How accurate/reliable was... Investigates whether media reports, government claims, or public perceptions truly matched the historical facts.
How successful was... Evaluates whether a specific law, treaty, or social protest achieved the goals it set out to reach.
How crucial/significant was... Explores the long-term importance of a single turning point or discovery on the modern world.
Which factors played a part in... Identifies the multiple causes: social, economic, and political, that led to a major international shift.
Did the introduction of... result in... Looks at the direct impact of a new technology, policy, or idea on the lives of people during these decades.
What was the contribution of... Focuses on how a specific group or individual helped change or influence their society during this timeframe.
What evidence was there to support... Challenges the researcher to find primary sources that prove or disprove a specific theory about the recent past.
Was there a correlation between... Analyzes if two trends (such as economic growth and social media use) happened together and influenced one another.
