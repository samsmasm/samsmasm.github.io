### Strategic Guide to the IB Business Management Internal Assessment

This briefing document synthesizes official IB requirements, examiner insights, and best practices for completing the Business Management Internal Assessment (IA). The IA is a business research project focused on a real-world issue or problem facing a specific organization.

#### Executive Summary

The IB Business Management Internal Assessment is a compulsory research project accounting for  **30% of the final grade for Standard Level (SL)**  and  **20% for Higher Level (HL)** . The fundamental requirements for both levels are now identical:

* **Word Count:**  Maximum of  **1,800 words** .  
* **Supporting Documents:**  Between  **three and five**  relevant, contemporary documents published within  **three years**  of the submission date.  
* **Conceptual Lens:**  The analysis must be conducted through one of four key concepts:  **Change, Creativity, Ethics, or Sustainability** .  
* **Focus:**  A real issue or problem facing a single, real-world business organization.  
* **Methodology:**  A combination of primary and/or secondary research analyzed using Business Management Tools, Theories, and Techniques (TTTs).Critical success factors include prioritizing the depth of analysis over the quantity of tools, ensuring the conceptual lens is integrated throughout the document, and selecting supporting documents that offer a range of balanced perspectives.

#### 1\. Core Requirements and Structure

The IA is intended to be a structured piece of writing that demonstrates the application of business skills to a real-organizational context.

##### 1.1 Technical Specifications

##### 1.2 Required Elements for Presentation

A well-presented project (Criterion G) must include:

* **Title Page:**  Must state the research question, the organization, the word count, and—crucially—the chosen  **Key Concept** .  
* **Table of Contents:**  Accurate and paginated.  
* **Structure:**  Appropriate headings and sub-headings (Criterion F).  
* **Numbered Pages.**  
* **Bibliography and Citations:**  Full referencing of all supporting documents and additional sources.

#### 2\. The Research Question and Conceptual Lens

The foundation of the IA is a well-defined research question (RQ) viewed through a specific conceptual lens.

##### 2.1 Selecting a Research Question

The RQ should be focused and specific rather than broad. It can be forward-looking (e.g., "Should Company X expand?") or backward-looking (e.g., "How effective was Company Y's strategy?").

* **Effective Example:**  "To what extent has Tesla's decision to cut prices impacted its market share and profitability in the US market?"  
* **Ineffective Example:**  "Improving customer satisfaction." (Too broad; lacks a specific timeframe or metric).

##### 2.2 Integration of the Key Concept (Criterion A)

Students must select  **one**  of the following four concepts:

1. **Change**  
2. **Creativity**  
3. **Ethics**  
4. **Sustainability**The student must go beyond mere definition; they must  **effectively integrate**  the analysis of the connection between the concept and the organization throughout the entire project. This concept must be explicitly stated on the title page to access the full 5 marks available for Criterion A.

#### 3\. Methodology: Supporting Documents (SDs)

The quality of the IA is heavily dependent on the selection of supporting documents (Criterion B).

##### 3.1 Selection Criteria

* **Relevance and Depth:**  Documents must directly address the RQ and provide enough data for in-depth analysis.  
* **Currency:**  Must be published within  **three years**  of the submission session (April or October of the final year).  
* **Balance:**  It is "strongly recommended" that SDs present a range of ideas and views. Using only company-published documents may lack the necessary objectivity.  
* **Quantity:**  3 to 5 documents. Fewer than three or more than five results in a score of only 1 mark for Criterion B.

##### 3.2 Types of Sources

* **Primary Research:**  Face-to-face/online interviews, surveys/questionnaires (include blank copies and summaries), and focus groups.  
* **Secondary Research:**  Press articles, business accounts, financial reports, mission statements, and government statistics.  
* **Audio/Video:**  A maximum of  **one**  SD may be a transcript of a video or audio file from a reliable organization.

#### 4\. Tools, Theories, and Techniques (TTTs)

Criterion C assesses the selection and application of business management tools. The IB emphasizes  **depth over quantity** .

##### 4.1 Selection Guidelines

While there is no "right" number, a recommendation of  **three to five TTTs**  is standard. Using more than five often leads to a superficial analysis that lacks the necessary depth for high marks.

* **Tools:**  Standard BMTs (15 for HL, 8 for SL) like the Ansoff Matrix or BCG Matrix.  
* **Theories:**  Frameworks like the 7Ps of marketing or motivation theories.  
* **Techniques:**  Quantitative calculations such as financial ratios (profitability, liquidity) or investment appraisal.

##### 4.2 Strategic Application of Common Tools

Tool,Best Practice Advice  
SWOT & STEEPLE,"Often used inappropriately. They are  descriptive  rather than analytical. Use them as supporting tools to provide context, but do not make them the main focus. Highlight only the most relevant parts."  
BCG Matrix,"A ""partial application"" is sufficient. Focus only on the parts of the matrix relevant to the business expansion or product in question."  
Financial Ratios,Essential for questions regarding profitability or market share to provide quantitative evidence.  
Porter's Five Forces,Useful for assessing the competitive environment and external threats.  
**Important Note:**  Avoid  **Force Field Analysis (FFA)** . While common in the old syllabus, it is generally considered inappropriate for the current syllabus requirements.

#### 5\. Analysis, Evaluation, and Conclusion

Criteria D and E focus on how the student uses evidence to reach a judgment.

##### 5.1 Analysis and Evaluation (Criterion D)

Students must effectively use data from the supporting documents. High-scoring IAs demonstrate:

* **Thorough Analysis:**  Moving beyond "regurgitating facts" to interpreting what themes emerge and how they help answer the RQ.  
* **Evaluation:**  Discussing the strengths and weaknesses of various positions and considering the assumptions and implications of the arguments.  
* **Visual Aids:**  Use of graphs, tables, and charts to visualize data and support points.

##### 5.2 Formulating Conclusions (Criterion E)

The conclusion must:

* Be  **consistent**  with the evidence presented in the main body.  
* **Explicitly answer**  the research question.  
* Avoid introducing new facts or arguments.  
* Acknowledge aspects of the RQ that remain unanswered or require further investigation.
