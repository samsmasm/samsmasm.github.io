News hunt
1. Read the news
Open Reuters, NY Times, The Guardian, news.google.com. Find three stories where a company is forced to make a big decision. Something specific. Aim for: "Starbucks faces union strikes in the US" or "Zara shifts production to Turkey to avoid shipping delays."


2. The STEEPLE "So What?"
What exactly is pushing the business to act? Is it a Legal change (new laws), an Economic shift (inflation), or a Technological breakthrough (AI)? If you can’t name the external pressure, it’s probably not right for an IA.


3. Pick Your Concept
Look back at the slides to really make sure it fits. Make sure it isn’t a square peg in a round hole. This is worth serious marks.


4. The "Working" Question
Fill in the blanks to see if it sounds like a real IA:
"Should [Company] [Do This Action] because of [The News Event]?"


Example:


The News: EU banning internal combustion engines by 2035.


The Question: Should BMW stop all petrol engine R&D by 2026 to stay ahead of EU regulations?


The Lens: Sustainability.


The "Can I actually do this?" Test:
Before you commit, google: "[Company Name] Annual Report 2023" or "[Company Name] Investor Relations." If you can't find their financial data or recent strategy PDFs in 30 seconds, pick a different company.
