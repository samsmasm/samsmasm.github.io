# FAQ

Here are some questions that students have asked that you should all have answer too. In no particular order...

## What are the grade boundaries?

The latest confirmed grade boundaries for the IA out of 25 marks are 20–25 for a 7, 17–19 for a 6, 13–16 for a 5, 10–12 for a 4, and 7–9 for a 3. But these can change each year. Frankly, does it matter? Aim as high as you can in each criteria!

## Do you need to include all 4 concepts?

No, you must select exactly one of the four key concepts to be your conceptual lens. Including more than one concept is a mistake that can lead to a score of zero for Criterion A.

## If you need more than 5 sources, can you use more?

You are strictly required to attach three to five supporting documents; attaching more than five will result in a lower mark for Criterion B. While you can reference additional materials like textbooks or class notes in your bibliography for context, these do not count as supporting documents. Additional materials can be referred to in passing and as minor supporting material, but your analysis should not rest on them.

## Do the sources need to be recent?

Yes, all supporting documents must be contemporary and published within a maximum of three years prior to your project's submission date. For IB 2027 students, your documents must be from May 2024 onwards.

## How do you recommend balancing the 5 supporting documents?

Aim for:

- A mix of internal and external source
- A range of types, i.e. financial reports and news articles and...
- A range of perspectives, i.e. opinions, different analyses.
- Different publications.

## How would you know that a secondary source is good/reliable?

Follow principles of checking reliability that you've learned in other classes for researching. Typically, look for documents published by the company itself, government agencies, academic journals, established market research companies, or recognized (mainstream) media. You should integrate comment on the reliability and potential limitations of your chosen documents within your analysis. You can also check with Sam!

## Does the case we're investigating need to be recent? How recent?

The IA must investigate a real and current business issue or problem facing a specific organization. Because your supporting documents are limited to a three-year window, the case itself must be contemporary enough to be documented by such recent evidence.
