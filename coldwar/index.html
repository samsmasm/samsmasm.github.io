OPERATION: SHADOW PROTOCOL

The Four Global Goals (Team Scoring)

  1. Counter-Intelligence: “Expose the Rats” — 20 Points. There is one Spy hiding in every team. You must correctly identify the Spies embedded in the other three nations. Observe rival behaviour. Who is whispering to other groups?
  2. The Courier: “The Infiltrator’s Memo” — 25 Points. Your nation has an elite Spy working inside a rival team. That Spy must obtain a copy of the rival team’s Manifesto and deliver it to their true home nation contact. The Spy must create a handwritten copy and pass it without the host team noticing.
  3. Information Security: “The Vault” — 30 Points. Your President will write a State Secret on a slip of paper and hide it within your team’s territory. Keep it hidden for the duration of the game. Use Decoy Secrets to mislead rivals.
  4. Asset Protection: “The State Icon” — 15 Points. Your team sculpts a State Icon from clay. Protect it until the end of the game. Earn 10 bonus points for stealing a rival’s Icon.

Individual Missions (Personal Scoring)

Each operative has one personal mission worth 5 Personal Points. Evidence must be provided to the Teacher.

Rules of Engagement

  1. The Shadow Rule: This game runs until the class before the Cold War assessment.
  2. Obey School Rules: No stealing by force. Use stealth, distraction, and social engineering. Do not go into others’ bags or laptops.
  3. The Teacher is the Oversight: All Intel Reports and Accusations must be whispered to the teacher or submitted in writing.
  4. Deniability: If accused of being a Spy, you must maintain your cover. Only the Teacher knows the truth.
  5. Counter-Sign Claims: If you trick a rival into saying your Counter-Sign, report it immediately by whispering to the teacher.
  6. The Double Agent’s Dilemma: If a Spy reaches the end of the game unidentified, they may choose which nation to share victory with — their official home nation or their true loyalty.
  7. The Trial: A nation may accuse one of their own of being a Spy. Report to the Teacher first. The class becomes Citizens of the Court — the accusing nation presents evidence, the accused defends themselves, the class acts as Jury, the Teacher is Judge. Correct guilty verdict: +10 points to accusing nation, convicted Spy is banished to the nation they were spying for. Wrongful conviction: −5 points. Maximum one Trial per nation.
